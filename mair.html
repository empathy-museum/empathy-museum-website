Community midwife

Mair initially did a degree in politics and worked in marketing for a construction company. It wasn’t until she had her own daughter that Mair experienced what a community midwife did, and its importance. After retraining, Mair worked for three months in a hospital as a midwife but didn’t enjoy it. For her, midwifery is very social, it’s about preparing people for their own families and leading the pregnant person’s care. It’s very hands-on – literally. Mair works part-time, combining her career as a community midwife with caring for her own children.
